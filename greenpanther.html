Introduction to Hydroponics

Hydroponics is a way to skip the soil, sub into a different material to support the roots of the plant, and grow crops directly in nutrient-rich water. In 1937, William Frederick Gericke is credited for his earliest modern reference to hydroponics. The Scientific reason behind this technology is that hydroponic systems actually use less water than traditional soil-based systems. This is because closed systems aren’t subject to the same rates of evaporation. Plus, the water used in hydroponic systems can be filtered, re-populated with nutrients, and fed back to plants again so that water is constantly recycled instead of wasted. This method can be inordinately helpful to states/cities that have poor land and are not able to sustain agriculture.

Introduction to growing medicinal plants hydroponically

Medicinal plants are increasingly cultivated on a commercial scale to satisfy the large demand for many natural remedies. These species are grown in an open field, which results in large year-to-year variability in both biomass production and the content of active principles. Hydroponic technology can be applied to produce high-standard plant material all year-round in consideration of the possibility to control growing conditions and to stimulate secondary metabolism by suitable manipulation of mineral nutrition.
